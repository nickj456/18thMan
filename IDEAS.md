# 18th Man — Product Ideas

A running list of ideas for future consideration. Not prioritised, not committed to — just captured.

---

## Collaboration

### ✅ Coach Groups
Allow coaches to form groups so they can share session plans privately with each other. Useful for coaching staffs at a single club, or regional development groups.

### ✅ Club Lockdown Mode
Option to restrict the platform (or a sub-section of it) to a specific club only — invite-only access, club-specific content, private drill libraries.

### Follow a Coach
Follow other coaches to get notified when they share new drills or sessions. Builds community and surfaces good content.

---

## Planning & Scheduling

### Calendar View
A calendar view for a coach (or coaching group) showing all planned sessions across the coming weeks. Makes it easy to see training load, gaps, and upcoming fixtures at a glance.

### Session Templates
Pre-built session structures a coach can clone and customise. Speeds up planning and gives newer coaches a starting point.

---

## Content & Drills

### ✅ Club-Specific Drills / Moves Section
A private drill section where a club can name drills and plays using their own internal terminology — e.g. what they call "The Scissors" at their club. Could sit behind a paid tier. Keeps proprietary moves private to the club.

### ✅ YouTube Channel Attribution
When a user adds a YouTube video to a drill, display the channel name on the drill card as attribution.

### Drill of the Week
An admin-curated or algorithmically picked featured drill shown prominently on the dashboard or drill library.

### Duplicate a Drill
Copy an existing drill as a starting point for a new one. Useful for creating variations of the same concept.

### Drill Preview on Hover
Quick peek at the canvas or video without navigating away from the drill library.

### Print / Share a Single Drill
Generate a shareable link or PDF for a single drill — useful for sending to assistant coaches or printing for training.

### Drill Comments
Currently drills only have ratings. Adding comments would allow coaches to discuss and give feedback on specific drills.

---

## Engagement & Gamification

### Weekly Drill Creation Target
Encourage coaches to create a set number of drills per week and reward them for hitting the target. The reward mechanic is TBD — options include streaks, badges, profile recognition, or unlocking features.

---

## Community & Profiles

### Profile Pages
Currently minimal. Showing a coach's public drills, club, groups, and coaching stats would make the community feel more real and encourage contribution.

### Global Search
Search across drills, sessions, coaches, and community threads from one place, rather than per-section filters.

---

## Onboarding & Retention

### Onboarding Flow
New users land on a blank dashboard with no guidance. A simple welcome flow directing them to create their first drill, join a club, or explore the library would reduce early drop-off.

### Email Notifications
Currently all notifications are in-app only. Email alerts for key events (club invites, group activity, new drills from followed coaches) would improve re-engagement.

---

## Monetisation

### Paid Tier
Monthly or yearly subscription to unlock premium features. Possible candidates for paid-only access:
- Club-specific drills / Moves section
- Coach groups / shared session plans
- Club lockdown mode
- Advanced analytics or session history

### ✅ PDF Export
Generate a clean, printable PDF of a session plan — layout, drill order, durations, coaching notes. Useful for handing to assistant coaches or printing for the training ground.

---

## UI & Experience

### ⬜ Light and Dark Mode
Toggle between light and dark themes across the app.

### Mobile-Friendly Drill Designer
The canvas designer is currently desktop only. A simplified mobile version would open up the tool to coaches working on the go.

### Admin Club Dashboard
Admins can see activity across their club — drills created, sessions planned, members active. Useful for club coordinators managing a coaching staff.
