# 18th Man — Presentation Agenda & Loose Script

---

## Agenda (20–25 min)

| # | Topic | Time |
|---|-------|------|
| 1 | Intro — the problem with coaching tools | 2 min |
| 2 | The AI coaching assistant (live demo) | 5 min |
| 3 | Coaching Blocks — plan your whole season | 6 min |
| 4 | Game Stats — live match tracking | 5 min |
| 5 | Everything else (quick tour) | 3 min |
| 6 | Sign up — scan the QR | 2 min |

---

## Loose Script

---

### 1. Intro (2 min)

> "Right, I want to show you something I've been building. It's called 18th Man — and it's a coaching platform built specifically for rugby league. Not generic sports software. Not something designed for union and bolted onto league. Built from scratch for how we coach.

> Before I show you the app, just a quick show of hands — how many of you plan your sessions in your head, or write it down the night before on a notepad? And how many of you have a folder of printed drills from years ago that you keep going back to?

> That's the problem. There are no good digital tools for rugby league coaches. Everything's either too complicated, too generic, or not built around the way we think about the game — the sets, the structures, the defensive patterns.

> I built 18th Man to fix that. Let me show you."

---

### 2. AI Coaching Assistant (5 min)

*Navigate to: Dashboard → Ask AI Coach*

> "This is the first thing I want to show you because it's the one that gets people. This is the AI coaching assistant. I'm going to ask it to plan me a session right now — live — and you can watch what comes back."

*Type this prompt live — don't pre-load it:*
> *"Plan a 60-minute training session for an U14s side, 12 players, focusing on handling under pressure. Give me a full timed run sheet with drills and coaching points."*

> "Watch this..."

*Let the response stream. Don't narrate over it — let them read it.*

> "That's not ChatGPT with 'rugby' on the label. It knows what a set of six looks like. It knows the Game Sense framework. It knows what U14s can and can't realistically do in a training session. And it gave us that in about ten seconds.

> You can ask it anything — tactics, player development, what to do when your wingers aren't connecting with the centres, how to structure a conditioning block. It speaks the language."

---

### 3. Coaching Blocks (6 min)

*Navigate to: Groups → [your group] → Coaching Blocks*

> "That was a one-off session. But what about your pre-season block? Your whole competition build-up? Most coaches — and I've been guilty of this — end up running the same focus two or three weeks in a row because you're busy, you're tired, you default to what you know.

> Coaching Blocks fixes that. You name the block, you tell it how many sessions you want, and the AI plans every single one upfront — balanced across Attack, Defence, Completions and Skills. Before you've stepped on the field."

*Show the block session list — point to the category colour coding.*

> "You can see here — each session has a focus area, it's already planned. A week before each session, you open it, check the AI's plan, add your own drills if you want, set a date. That's it.

> And here's the bit I really like — if the team had a bad game on the weekend and your set completion fell apart, you can swap sessions. The rotation stays intact, you just pull forward the completions session. The whole block adapts."

*Open a session and show the prepared plan.*

> "This is the squad page as well — players, notes, ratings. All your coaching knowledge in one place, not lost in a notebook."

---

### 4. Game Stats (5 min)

*Navigate to: Groups → [your group] → Game Stats*

> "Now the match itself. Who here tracks carries and tackles on the sideline? How are you doing it — paper, mental notes?

> This is Game Stats. It runs on your phone. One tap per event — carries, tackles, set completions. That's it. No clipboard, no trying to remember at half time."

*Show tally mode — tap a few events.*

> "Then when the match is done, you flip to the review. You get workload percentage per player — so you can see straight away if your six is doing 30% of the carries. Half-by-half breakdown. Tries and conversions. And you can export it as a PDF for your records."

*Switch to review mode.*

> "This is the kind of data professional clubs have had for years. We can have it too."

---

### 5. Quick Tour (3 min)

> "There's more in here — let me quickly show you a couple of things."

*Navigate to: Drills*

> "Drill library — community drills you can browse, save, and rate. Plus a designer where you can draw your own drills on a digital pitch canvas. Drag and drop players, cones, arrows, movement patterns. If you've got a drill in your head you've been running for years, you can finally get it out of your head and into something you can share."

*Navigate to: Community*

> "Community board — coaches sharing what's working at training. You can post questions, share session ideas, discuss tactics. Early days but it'll grow."

> "There's also session planning, player wellbeing check-ins, game plans, weekly focus topics you can push to the whole squad. It's all in here."

---

### 6. Sign Up (2 min)

*Navigate to: 18thman.app/coaches — leave it on screen*

> "So here's how you get on. I've set up access through our club so you'll be on the full plan straight away — all the features, no restrictions.

> Scan the QR code or go to the URL on screen. Takes two minutes to sign up. When you're in, join the club and you'll see everything I've just shown you.

> Any questions?"

---

## Tips for the night

- **Pause after the AI streams.** Let the room read it. Don't rush to explain.
- **Ask the show-of-hands questions.** It gets people leaning in before you even open the app.
- **Don't go through every feature.** The five beats above are enough. Less is more.
- **Leave the /coaches page on screen during Q&A.** People will scan it while you're talking.
- **If the internet drops** — have the AI chat pre-loaded with the session plan response already shown.
